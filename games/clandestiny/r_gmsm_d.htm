Nice Touches:

The quirky characters did amuse me for a while. The background music is often comprised of bagpipes, of course, which does help with the games "regional authenticity." I do have to comment on the cute movie bits that occur periodically which help to enhance the adventure's playability, but they do not make the game.

Not-So-Nice Touches:

 

 The game has a feeling of being a child's adventure game, it's too silly and lacks maturity. However, playing at the average intelligence level, people under fifteen I think would be unable to grasp many of the puzzles. Another unfavorable aspect of this game is the constant reoccurrence of Fergus, the castle's handyman, who keeps popping up at the entrance of every door, to tell your character that he has the key to the door--the knowledge. Enough already, a couple of appearances would suffice. Also, why is it when Andrew asks for a tip (you can receive four before the computer will solve the puzzle for you), rarely is it a helpful hint, but another puzzle. Shouldn't a hint be a helpful and reasonably easy to interpret? It would seem that you need a hint to help solve the tip.
