One would think that if I enjoyed The 11th Hour then Clandestiny (whose interface this game closely resembles) would be just as enjoyable, but it's not. Why? First, the characters are not nearly as appealing. In Clandestiny, the characters are childish and more often than not, annoying and long-winded. Secondly, the storyline is not nearly as appealing nor adventurous. As a player, you should feel driven to finish the game. Clandestiny, makes time stand still, and not in a good way. Lastly, the producers of Clandestiny paid close attention to the animation and the Scottish accents, but pay little attention to the storyline and the intelligence of the gamer. In comparing this game to other recent adventure-style games, for example Titanic, Clandestiny bored me rather than enthralled me.

Audio/Video:

Clandestiny has all the graphics that should constitute a great adventure game. The graphics are clear and crisp,

resembling a classic Walt Disney cartoon movie. The majority of characters are true Scotsmen and women with their thick Scottish accents and quirky humor. However, if you are someone with Scottish heritage, you may find that the stereotypes get a little redundant and silly after an hour or so.
