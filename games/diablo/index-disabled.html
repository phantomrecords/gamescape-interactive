Gamesmania
Review
"Diablo took me completely by surprise. Naturally I have become hopelessly addicted to Blizzard's Warcraft series. I expected that sort of real-time speed and strategy that makes Warcraft II such a classic. Since you're not directing units of troops, but one character, I was ready for something arcade-ish. Little did I realise that I would soon sink my teeth into a game that delivers the entertainment of a good long-haul Role-Playing Game. [ What The Advertisers Say] In Diablo, you choose one of three character types: [ The Warrior] [ The Rogue Archer] or [ The Sorcerer] and, using a decimated town as a base of operations, slowly infiltrate a cursed cathedral. The cathedral is randomly generated each time you start a new game, but there is an automap to help you keep track of your current dungeon. The townspeople will occasionally assign you tasks (there's a quest log so you can keep your mission in mind), but you can explore the dungeon on your own time if you wish, gathering treasure and building up your character for the real fight that you know is waiting for you on the lower levels. That's right. It's a good old-fashioned dungeon-crawl, but put together with Blizzard's style and talent."

Gamezilla (standard)
Review
"If you've played Diablo, reading my review will simply be confirmation of something you already know: this game rocks. We here at Gamezilla try to avoid such phrases because they tend to get overused and fail to convey real specifics. But in this case, I can't avoid saying it: Diablo truly rocks. If you know this, your time is probably better spent playing Diablo than reading this...if you want to see what all the fuss is about, I'll do my best to clue you in. Diablo, in my opinion, is the first completely successful combination of role-playing and real-time action to hit the PC. There have been successful RPGs and great action games, but never a combination that played so easily and offered so much as Diablo. Until now, RPGs were basically a niche, mostly bought and played by diehard Dungeons and Dragons types. Similarly, action games have traditionally been focused on inducing an adrenaline rush, with only the occasional half-hearted attempts to incorporate role-playing or character-building elements."

Review (talks about playing Diablo in multiplayer mode)
"Let me start out by saying that Diablo is the first game that I would even consider playing only multiplayer, without even trying the single player version. I do not say this because single player is somehow flawed or less than excellent. In fact, I heartily enjoyed single player, and would have been pretty satisfied by this game even if it had no multiplayer abilities. However, the multiplayer aspect of this game has everything in the single player game, except for the random quest generator (why not?) and the option to save and restore as you please (keeping down on the level of multiplayer cheating). But then again, you already know how good this game is, and that is why you are reading this article. Let me get down to business, and since the focal point for all this gaming action is Battle.net (over 100,000 gamers use it already!), I will center my suggestions on that little online gaming community, although most of them apply to multiplaying in general."

Jim Bob
Player Review
"9.5 out of 10. Diablo is one awesome game. The graphics are nice, (you can zoom in to see more gore) the game play is perfect, and the strategy is there too. But the thing that really makes Diablo a winner in my opinion is the game play. The difficulty curve of the dungeon levels is just right - when you've cleared the first level, your character is strong enough for the second level. Blizzard have recognised that this is the most important aspect of a dungeon-exploring RPG, and thye've got it just right. Its still enough of a challenge for you to require constant saving, and maybe even to start a new game (equipment and experience carried over) to redo those first levels to make your character stronger for the later levels. Don't pass over this game until you've experienced the IMMENSE satisfaction received from splatting those skeletons."
