Don't expect the constant crazy action of Warcraft fighting or racing to control resources. Don't expect the tremendous depth of Betrayal at Krondor. Instead, expect an excellent mix of both, for just the right blend of fighting action, and RPG quest. And it's all animated beautifully, and it sounds lovely. Add to that a smooth interface that does all the work but lets you make the decisions (though it occasionally suffers from Warcraft's "No wait! I didn't want you to go there!" panics), you've got one addictive little mother of a game. Play this one, and you'll find yourself thinking about it constantly. You'll be at work or standing in a check-out line thinking about how much more gold you need to buy a magic axe, or how you should think about boosting your magic abilities with your next level up.

Warcraft cost me some late nights of feverish play. Check out Diablo and see if you don't catch yourself thinking "My God, I've got to

get up and go to work in two hours...but I only need forty more gold pieces..."
