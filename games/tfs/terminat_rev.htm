Oct-26-96 REVIEW: Although the controls took time to master, Terminator Future Shock is a hellishly addictive 3D game. Of course there will be others as so but at the moment, "There is only one!" But, hey, don't take my word for it... read the two examples below and judge for yourself:

 

Video Clip Here...

Terminator Future Shock

While running through battle-devastated Los Angeles, I come across an overturned bus repeatedly. I've been plundering L.A. for a good hour now and I'm getting the feeling that this bus is there for a reason. (Actually I blew it up and then realised I needed it.) So I went and jumped onto the bus, a low roof, across a fallen metal beam, and voilã, power-ups galore.

On another occasion I knew of a Terminator hiding behind a door (because it blasted me last time!) I sorted just the solution though! So here goes... I opened the door then ran like hell back up the hall, and around a corner! Since the Terminator is slow he'll have to take damage coming after me! But there's more. My character, you see, throws grenades and other projectiles from the left hand. So by straffing just barely out to the hallway from the right, my body isn't exposed but my throwing arm is. And you know what that means... I launched grenade after grenade until I his pieces hit the ground... Ah, such a tidy plan.

There are always moments like this in Future Shock and that's what makes it so much fun. The machines aren't boorishly stupid though. You see when the robots in this game get shot they start moving towards the direction of fire. So even if you catch one sleeping it starts towards you the moment it gets hit. It'll stop after a few yards if it doesn't track you or isn't taking damage but it's certainly not dumb. So there is a real combat of intellects going on here that ought to keep anyone entertained who can appreciate a good mix of strategy, action, and a sense of being there.
