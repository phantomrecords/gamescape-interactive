When you first install the software, you must select an egg from the "hatchery" (a floppy disk that contains six eggs - once these are gone, they're gone). Then, the egg is placed in the Norn's world, a large environment similar to a fish-tank or a big hamster cage and filled with food, toys, dangerous things and even a nasty critter (the Grendel) - all of which your Norn can interact with. You can even teach your Norns to speak, by associating words with various objects and actions and repeatedly saying them. This can be maliciously fun, as there is nothing to stop you teaching your Norn rude words instead of "pull", "sleep", "carrot" etc, which provides many hours of immature amusement as your Norns describe simple and innocuous actions with all the charm and grace of a HGV truck driver.

Initially, Creatures is hugely compelling and captivating. Watching your Norns grow from little crawling babies to old and forgetful pensioners (the

average Norn "lives" for 40 hours) is fun, as is teaching them to play with all the toys and interactive objects (pianos, drums, slide projectors - the list is long and varied). There is no doubt that the programming involved is an amazing feat, and that the claims Creatures made are actually correct - these little things have a (a-)life of their own...
