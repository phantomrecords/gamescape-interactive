In the future, we live in a couch potato's utopia, where our biomechanical creations do all the menial tasks in life, such as scrubbing the toilets. But thinking such duties are crappy at best, these mechanical maids turn on their masters and begin unleashing their aggressions throughout the city.

Your assignment: control as many as 3 RATs (Remote Assault Tanks) through a 25-mission campaign to annihilate these rankled robots and restore peace.

 Bedlam will be very familiar to those who've spent anytime playing the Crusader games. Like both Crusaders, Bedlam does not feature a multiplayer mode. It does improve on the Crusader model, however, by omitting the slow pace and complicated plot elements. From your skewed, top-down perspective you comb the area looking for something to shoot at, blow up, and/or destroy. Here the thing to remember is the more pyrotechnics, the better. Nearly everything in Bedlam blows up really well, and there's plenty of unique weapons that cause explosions: bombs, bouncy grenades, photon guns, sticky grenades, and more.

This is not to say the game is a total trigger fest, devoid of puzzles or brain requirements. While distributing the wrath of your weaponry on the rebellious robots, be on the lookout for teleporters to unlock off-limits areas, pressure pads to raise and lower sections, and switches to turn off force
