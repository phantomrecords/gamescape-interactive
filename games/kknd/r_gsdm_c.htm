Unlike Warcraft 2, instead of using the higher resolution to put more detail into the units and structures, the sprites are quite small so that you can see more of the playing field on screen at once. On the plus side, there is noticeably much less slow down when lots of units are moving, than with RA.

KKnD hosts quite a large and varied selection of buildings and units for both sides, all of which are drawn

very professionally, with great imagination. The backdrops too, are realistically post-apocalypse, and add nicely to the overall theme of the game. One of the main (but hotly debated) criticisms of RA was that the two warring sides were unevenly matched - KKnD seems to have chosen the Warcraft 2 route to avoid that one, by making each side's units generally hold the same defensive and offensive attributes (with a few exceptions).

There's a wide scope of

different objectives to accomplish in each of the missions throughout KKnD. In fact, I was very surprised at how few of the first 6 or 7 missions actually required you to establish and build up your base; most involved you starting with a set number of troops, and then moving them out to either protect convoys, destroy all enemy presence or rescue stranded soldiers.
