The success of a sequel to anything is never certain until the moment of its release. However, in the case of Civilization II, or Civ II as most fans will probably refer to it, I doubt that Sid Meier and his talented crew ever had a thing to fear. It may sound the same as the original Civ, but Sid Meier has added a few touches that make playing this game just as addictive, if not more so, than the original. For starters, the graphics have improved significantly. Gone are the static colour squares with a number representing the number of citizens in a given city. Now, we have graphic representations of huts, towers, and buildings that grow in size and sophistication as your society advances. The same goes for the mobile units which strike out into parts unknown to search out new lands, hostile enemies, and potential allies. There are more combat choices, such as marines, paratroopers, various ships and aircraft, and missiles such as nuclear bombs. Normally, I'm a peaceful, non-confrontational person, but I have to admit that when the Zulus got out of hand and wouldn't respect my borders, the nuclear fallout and resulting mushroom cloud over one of their cities did give me a guilty twinge of satisfaction.

So, for all you potential Caesars, Lincolns, or Ghengis Khans who have dreamed at one time of ruling the world, this is the game for you. For all the others who have purchased the original Civilization, you will not be disappointed. A solid
