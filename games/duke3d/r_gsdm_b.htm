Of course, realistic levels are all very well, but mean nothing if the level design's crap. Well, thankfully, DN3D manages to hold it's own in that area. There are all manner of things to jump on and duck behind, there are darkened corridors, places to conceal nasty monsters. A subway train turns up on one level. It's difficult to say exactly what it is that makes a good level, but 3D Realms seem to have cracked it. Each level has a

number of features which fit in with that location. Enter the cinema on the first level, and you'll find monsters popping out from behind the seats. Blow them away, and you're safe, or are you? There's a smashing sound, and you'll find that the baddies in the projection room have smashed the window and are now blasting away at you. Help!

Caught on the plop

And that's not all. There are toilets (where were those

in Doom, eh?), where you can relieve yourself for a health bonus, or if you're feeling particularly destructive, blow up the urinals, and smash the mirror. Open the cubicle doors, and you might just find a bad guy doing his business. A blast from the shotgun, and he's dead before he can reach the bog roll. The levels are be-decked with features and effects like this. Go into the sushi-bar and you'll find a karaoke mike, which you can sing into, and even a food-conveyor, with assorted body parts. Yum!
