Eventually, the process of rearing a Norn and guiding it through its life becomes so routine as to end up a little tedious. It is also slightly distressing when they prove to be amazingly incestuous and immoral beasts, meaning a Norn family tree looks more like a spirograph design. And the environment is perhaps just a little bit too large, especially when you're trying to keep track of four or five creatures, all in different parts of their world.

In conclusion, no-one is disputing the incredible programming techniques on display here and Creatures is an attractive and initially compelling desktop toy. However, there is no game here as such and after a few hours you will find yourself tiring of it a little, eventually giving it up for something a little more addictive and rewarding
