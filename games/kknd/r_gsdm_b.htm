These two basically have quarrels with one another for the same reason most of today's cultures are constantly at war. They have different backgrounds, and believe in different God's. You get to play on either side, through a campaign of 15 missions. Each level is linked into with a preceeding FMV cutscene, where your commander briefs you on the upcoming mission.

The quirky humor in these scenes adds a certain charm to the game, and takes away the morbid seriousness of the

situation; if you're into a sincerely atmospheric war-like simulation, then you should definitely look elsewhere. The whole game is a spoof; one Survivor mission has you rescuing a dorky bottom-ranked private who got lost, while the text at the side of the screen (typed by an unknown source) reads, "let's just leave the idiot out there... what a nerd." I actually let out a burst of laughter when the Evolved leader started waving a human head at me, spouting on about how the enemy looks different from them ("the symmetricals", as we're better known, which makes sense considering a lot of the Evolved seem to be missing certain facial features we all take for granted) and must be destroyed.
