Diablo's saved game system. You can only save one game per character at a time. I think many gamers, particularly RPG lovers, might prefer a system where they could recall older games, particularly just before the player is required to make a character-building decision. It would be very convenient to save the game just before you spend ability points, especially if you got deep into a dungeon and realized that extra Dexterity instead of Strength might be helpful, or that choosing the super-duper weapon over the super-duper armor was a mistake. It's possible, however, that this is actually what Blizzard had in mind when they designed this game.

Manual/Hint Book:

Blizzard has never gone lightly with their manuals. This one is packed with information, including a lengthy background story, descriptions of monsters and characters, and lots of useful gameplay information. There are four pages on how to setup a multiplayer game alone. The manual is also

nicely illustrated with art familiar to anyone who has tried to command Blizzard's armies of Orcs and Humans.

Summary:

The Case For: Gotta play addictiveness, excellent multiplayer support.

The Case Against: The townsfolk could use some more depth.

The Verdict: Fantastic game. I predict many late nights down in the dungeon for me.
