Graphics:

Diablo is played from an isometric perpective, like Origin's Crusader: No Remorse. One of Blizzard's nice touches is the partial transparency of walls down screen from your character. This allows you to easily scan for objects or doors, and you never "lose" your character behind a wall like in some

games (Total Mayhem for instance). This means that no matter what nook or cranny you crawl into, you'll always be able to see where you are. In a game where you fight by clicking the mouse on your opponents, this is vital. The graphics themselves are gorgeously rendered in SVGA, and you have the option to zoom in on the action if you like or stay back for a wider view of your surroundings.
