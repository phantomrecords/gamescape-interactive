RA lacked. Firstly, instead of putting up with a huge status bar that eats away a quarter of the right side of the screen, KKnDsquare uses small buttons that then extend into more buttons when you click on one. This stands as a major organisation improvement - I would often waste valuable seconds (even minutes!) scrolling through the endless list of units/structures in RA. This was due to the game only being able to display 4 of the items on screen at once, in just 2 columns, out of a huge range of 20 or more. The buttons in KKnD are divided into three sections (infantry, vehicles, buildings), and practically all options can be displayed at once, making implementation of building new units and structures a much quicker process. Additionally, by holding the right mouse button and dragging the mouse, you can 'quick scroll' all around the map, which is very handy when you have no radar.

Unfortunately though, there were many things missed out that severely decreases the interface's ease of use, and forces you to spend more time

manually directing troops, instead of using careful planning and thinking up new strategies. These include: not being able to send multiple troops in for repair, no visual confirmation when assigning groups to a number, can't 'bookmark' areas on the map, no troop commands like 'Guard', 'Formation', 'Patrol' and 'Protect' as implemented in RA, and finally, no option to 'Force Move' so running over and squishing enemy troops can only be accomplished by commanding big vehicles to move somewhere beyond them
