Nice Touches:

Unlike most games on the market, Civ II realizes that not all gamers are male. For each civilization the gamer has the option of being either male or female, and offers a historically significant man or woman from each of the cultures offered in the game. It's so nice not to be alienated.

 Not-So-Nice Touches:

I've tried to be objective about this, but to be completely honest I can't spot a problem with the game. In the innumerable hours I've spent on this game, I have yet to find something to complain about.

Manual/Hintbook:

With most games these days eighty bucks will get you a CD and a registration card, but contrary to popular procedure, Microprose has

once again included an instruction manual that puts most other gaming manuals to utter shame. One hundred and eighty-three pages of detailed description, explanation, and advice come with the game. The Sid Meier games are the only ones I've ever picked up and wondered if I strained something because the box was so heavy.
If more help is needed for strategy the official manual will be out for around $28 US.
