Gamespot
Review
"Bedlam isn’t a great or brilliant game: it won’t blind you with its originality or searing gameplay. But it’s a decent enough game with a fair number of fun hours in it, and that in itself can be rare enough these days. Bedlam is a faster, more mindless version of the Crusader/Syndicate school of gaming: top-down, hard-core hellraisin’. Its premise suits it well. In the future, we have come to rely on biomechanical creatures for our menial labor. Now these creatures have run amok and are raging across the city, lowering property values wherever they go."

Review
"Once you get Bedlam installed and started up (no mean task in itself), you're greeted by some terrific intro scene animation. Giant blimp-like spacecraft fly past your viewpoint. Edward Govednik
Player Review
"Bedlam has all of the makings of a good Crusader/Syndicate sequel. Its movement is fluid and fast! No more clicking on multiple locations to move your character a few feet on screen. Fast and furious would describe the intensity of the game. On both a Pentium 90 and 166, the graphics don't overload the system while giving a great level of detail, though sometimes confusing. The overall story was interesting: mutant dogs and robots going crazy and you and your team have to stop them. Granted, its not the most original story line seen, but when it comes to action shoot em ups, it falls on the same lines with Syndicate. Gameplay is fun and that's what gaming is all about. If you liked Origin's Crusader or BullFrog's Syndicate, you'll like Bedlam. Mayhem at its finest!" [ Go To The Source Of This Review]
