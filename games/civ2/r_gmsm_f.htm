The game runs great under Windows 3.1 or Windows 95 with a suggested minimum of a 486 DX2/66 with 2X CD ROM and 8 MB RAM. I found that the game worked just as well with a 486 SX/66, but I would strongly recommend a 4X CD ROM so that the video flow is smoother and faster. As well, if you have 16 MB RAM you'll be in luck since an optional graphics program is available to you. With 16 RAM the game will show you a virtual representation of your human rivals and allies and an indication of their relative wealth and power. If you're like me and aren't fortunate enough to have this much power under the hood, don't worry; the game is just as exciting without it.

Summary:

The Case For: Improved everything, with the playability of the first game intact.

The Case Against: Playability may make your employer suspicious of the amount of time you call in

"sick".

The Verdict: Quite possibly the most innovative and addictive computer game of all time.
