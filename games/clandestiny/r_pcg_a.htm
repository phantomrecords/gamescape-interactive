Clandestiny's animated sequences replace the occasional live action of earlier releases. [ Screen Shot 1 ] Frankly, you'll want to skip them, but alas, some of the displayed information becomes improtant to your progress. These sequences are poorly written, overacted, and full of tacky animation. In fact, they uncannily reproduce the atsmosphere of Scooby-Doo. That says it all.

The music is by turns moody, atmospheric, and wittily evocative. Clandestiny's

humor is wild and silly, appearing from time to time in the voice-over narration and the tongue-in-cheek Guidebook itself.

It's safe to say the Trilobyte's worked its magic again in Clandestiny. This game is great puzzle-based fun as superb a display of contemporary 3D graphics as you could ask for.
