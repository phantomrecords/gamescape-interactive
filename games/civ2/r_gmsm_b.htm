Audio/Video:

Once again the game is played with an overhead view perspective with improved graphics. Cities, terrain, and mobile units are rendered in finer detail and colour than the original Civ. Though I must say that one of the most exciting additions to Civ II is the video clips that accompany the Wonders of the World advancements

available to the player. When the player has successfully built a Wonder, such as the Colossus of Rhodes, or the Pyramids, the system kicks in with a mini-movie, each with its own unique and well-played theme music. These clips include actual film footage, historical drawings, writings, or still photos of ancient artifacts. Needless to say, the first time I saw the Great Wall of China on my 486 SX/66 I was quite blown away. As well, Microprose has included a Throne Room feature which will become more plush and decadent as you become more successful and make your citizens happier.

Storyline:

This strategy game possesses a very simplistic story line; it's the journey you, the gamer, take from the very beginning of your culture's civilization to the point where you successfully colonize another planet.
