"Maim, blast, squish, stomp. Again. Well, it's that time again. While ID sycophants the world over are trembling in anticipation of Quake, some software house has seen fit to chuck out yet another Doom clone, with a few bells and whistles added. Duke Nukem 3D is Apogee's latest contribution to the genre. The shareware version has been out for a few months, and already the usual 'Quake's gonna blow it away' 'No it isn't' arguments have started cropping up across all the newsgroups. Then there's the hopelessly pedantic 'It's 3D' 'No it isn't!' 'Yes it bloody is!' postings. And the constantly changing release date. So it was with some trepidation that I approached the whole Duke Nukem 3D thing."

Anonymous March 18, 1997
Player Review
"Duke is better than Quake. For one thing it has clearer color. Quake has way too many shades of earth tones and it is way easier to play a game of Duke over the modem than Quake. I haven't got Quake's modem to modem play to even work yet and it is very annoying to shell out $50 U.S. for a product that doesn't perform as promised. Quake was supposed to make it easy to play multiplayer games, but I guess this didn't include modem to modem games. They probably figured that wasn't important seeing how they hyped up the Quake Internet thing. But anyway, Internet play is too slow with both Duke and Quake so you can't even factor this into the equation. If anyone knows how to get faster Internet play with a 28.8 modem, please enlighten me. As a standalone game, I can run Duke in 640x480 with no problems, but Quake is about half that resolution. Quack has its moments, but if I was stranded on a desert island I'd wish for the Duke."

 

Requirements

Windows 95 or NT 4.0, Pentium 60MHz, 8MB RAM, double speed CD-ROM drive, Windows 95 compatible sound card and mouse, local bus SVGA video card (DirectX compatible).

Multiplayer Options: 16MB RAM, 2 players - 144400 BPX modem or null modem cable, 2-4 players - IPX network or battle.net (requires low-latency internet connections with support for 32-bit application).
