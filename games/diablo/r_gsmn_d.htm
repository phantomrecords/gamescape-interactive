Compare To:

To some extent, I reminded of the old quarter-muncher Gauntlet, and a 3rd person version of the Ultima Underworld series. The world view is somewhat reminiscent of Origin's Crusader series, although the

settings are light years apart. Warcraft will not leap into your head immediately when you see Diablo. The easy-to-learn mouse interface and fantasy setting are the only real similarities, unless you count the excellent overall quality of Diablo.

Nice Touches:

If the Diablo interface was any more user-friendly, you'd invite it to your wedding. Dragging your cursor over any item or menu option causes the message

window to display the all the relevant information about it. It will tell you what the object does, how to use it (such as right-clicking or pressing a hot key), if the item has been damaged, and so on. Your character stats screen is wonderful. All the necessary statistics are there immediately. I find the counter that shows when you'll advance to a new level particularly useful.
