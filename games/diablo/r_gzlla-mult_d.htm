Once you are in a game, simply pressing enter or pushing the mouth button will bring up the chat window. Type your message and press the enter key, and your message will be broadcast. When you bring up the chat window, there should be a list of up to three players, each with a mute button beside their names. If you press the mute button, you can still hear what a person says, but they can't hear you. Remember that since the talk window pops up over the item description window, if you are telling your buddy about the sword you just found, you might need to write the message piece by piece, using small sentences so you can display the item's list of stats in between statements.

As you go deeper into the dungeon, you will find that certain areas are so difficult that you can not handle them alone. At that point, you have two options. First of all, you can avoid the seedy areas until your character is strong enough, or you can take a well-mixed party of

adventurers down with you. Because of the different balances between characters, you will find that the second option is usually the better one.

For instance, a Warrior has the strength and power to go toe-to-toe with most of the enemies, but has little options in the form of long-distance attacks, so swarms of archers or spellcasters can be a nightmare.
