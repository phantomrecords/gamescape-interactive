Every door's riddle answer appears somewhere in the motto, and the more doors you open the easier the puzzles are to solve. The first room you can enter after solving a door puzzle is the dinning room. Head there first, it is near the games room. Do not forget to check every room for a throbbing brain which indicates the discovery of a puzzle. Also, be sure to carefully look at all the pictures on the wall, some of them talk and others will solve puzzles for you. The only other hint you need for the first part of the game is the answer for the library entrance. The answer is Scotland (the puzzle is very misleading). Some of the puzzles are very obscure and silly and will no doubt thoroughly frustrate you. Don't worry, just move on or let the computer solve it for you.

Playability:

The interface is slow and annoying, clearly resembling

The 11th Hour. Navigation is accomplished by using the mouse, rarely is the keyboard used. Depending on the level of difficulty you choose, solving the puzzles is ranked accordingly. There is no use of inventory items and little interaction with the three-dimensional environment.

Compare To:

Not only does this game resemble The 11th Hour, much of the interface is exactly the same with only a few minor exceptions.
