I'm also quite displeased with the standard of AI on your own troops. Westwood weakened, but didn't completely crush, the barrier that most real-time strategy programmer's face when trying to get troops to move to a place specified by the player, and actually get there! KKnD seems to have taken a slight step backwards. I'd tell a group of 10 or so to get to the other side of a bridge or through a narrow valley, and

would they make it? Maybe. Eventually. They'd jump about, lots of reversing, getting out of each other's way - they couldn't seem to figure if they simply got into single-file things would go a lot quicker. Instead they all go at once, all bump into one another, all say, "oh excuse me, after you ... no no, after you ... no, I insist..."

And the very worst part of your own team's AI - simply put, they're dumb! It seems impossible to position a group of troops to defend a building in a place where they will all defend sensibly. All

your units seem unaware of anything that's going on around them. If an enemy comes along and attacks the first unit it comes across, then that soldier will fight back, but what will the 20 standing literally one meter away do? Sweet FA, basically. Check their watches, and talk about the weather. This is very frustrating because it usually has you frantically scanning all over the map, being forced to manually tell every unit to advance and attack when their teammates are being pummelled into the dirt.
