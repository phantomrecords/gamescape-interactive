Dave Smith
Player Review
"Thank you for Red Alert, it was delivered promptly and has turned out to be well worth the money. I can't believe I was going to pay more for it at "D-- Sm--h Electronics", and I would have had to go and pick it up myself! I have just conquered the world as the General of the Soviet side and thought I had better tell someone about it. The graphics as I'm sure you know are much better than the infamous C&C, the scroll rates are higher and smoother and run very nicely on my 133mhz, 16meg, 8x cdrom machine. The video footage that precedes every mission is fantastic, my personal favorite is of an allied tank being whammied from behind by a pair of missiles launched from a mean MIG 29 (in racing red of course!) that whistles through a valley at HIGH speed. The story line is better than C&C, and the opening sequence that gives the gist of 'why' things are the way they are is superb. Additionally the game-engine or whatever it's called is smart and is not easily fooled by bluff counter-attacks or sending the 'pawns' in to die while the nobility takes out the really vital stuff. The computer opponent responds to weaknesses that may be created but ceases to attack if attack is no longer profitable. The longest mission I ever played was the second to last, when I just could not see an opening and was constantly on the defence; however, after 7 hours I finally whipped his tail. This game is the one for anybody who is into chess, Civ' or Sim City games. You have full license to print what you want from this reveiw, because I think eveyone needs a copy. So having said that when can I testdrive 'Skynet' or maybe a Flight sim - I have the Sidewinder Pro joystick that needs regular exercise if you know what I mean! Talk to you soon."
