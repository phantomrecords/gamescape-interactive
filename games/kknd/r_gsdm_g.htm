The interesting weapons and creatures, along with the cool setting, and well-designed multiplayer maps sum up to make very worthy competition for the other multiplay strategy titles. Unfortunately, and probably the reason for the game's failing to cause a great stir in the online gaming community, playing over the Net is pretty much impossible on a standard 33.6 modem. If Red Alert can do it so smoothly, then KKnD certainly could have; they just didn't bother optimising it. Conclusion

There seems to be many bad points in this review, that severely outweigh the good. This is unfair really, as KKnD is a quality game. It stands out from the crowd with great graphics, well presented storyline, a cute humor that shows they never took the plot very seriously (not a bad thing!), and a terrific range of units and structures that vary on both sides. It also plays well in multiplayer, with both the Survivors and Evolved being pretty even in battle.

The problem seems to be

just a combination of small faults. Along with the difficult missions, and the slightly inefficient and dated AI and interface, KKnD advances nothing in the 6 months that have passed since RA was released. If you're tired of waiting for the big releases like Rebellion and Starcraft that promise to be nothing less than spectacular, then KKnD provides a very nice filler.
