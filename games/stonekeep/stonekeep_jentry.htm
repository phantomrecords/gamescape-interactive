Journal Entry for Stonekeep: Saturday, March 8, 1997

Stonekeep

Having just finished playing an old StoneKeep demo I thought I should make a journal entry about the game in case anyone else was wondering. I watched this game on the shelves of game stores in the U.S. and considered buying it many times. I never actually did and a fortuitous decision that was because the gameplay is "a little less than impressive."

The game is by Interplay who made Out Of This World (a very good game, even today because it's challenging and creative but playable - and fits on just two floppies). But it seems that StoneKeep wasn't bestowed with such pleasantries. Gameplay is similar to Eye Of The Beholder by TSR, but has movie cut-scenes, etc. instead of the comic-book scenes found in Eye Of The Beholder. Also the interface is confusing, although Interplay felt it was liberating. The creatures are also blocky (even for animation standards from 1995) and don't offer much in the way of creepiness. I found the dialogue more reminiscent of a B-grade movie than anything else I can recall at the moment.

So needless to say I wouldn't recommend ye ol' Keep even though it is old so sells for a low price. The fact is there are many other great games of this type selling for even less or free from the folks who want to trade a few for something current. And to name a few: Eye Of The Beholder, SpellCaster, or Dungeon Hack, come to mind. All older and all cheap these days.

Well that's all for me on StoneKeep.
