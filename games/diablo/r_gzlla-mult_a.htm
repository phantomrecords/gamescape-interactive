Surviving:

The first thing you want to do in Diablo is to get a decent character worked up. If you plan on playing multiplayer as much as many people, you will eventually have a very high level character, so you should plan for it. While I feel that all three character classes are well balanced, it seems that Warriors have an advantage at the beginning, Rogues in the middle, and Sorcerers towards the end. As a result, I would lean towards creating a sorcerer

character if you plan on using it for the long term, but keep in mind that sorcs tend to be quite weak in the early levels. All three character classes have their own strengths and weaknesses, and all three are crucial to a good multiplayer crusade.

Single Play in Multiplayer Mode:

One of the common strategies for getting a weak character up quickly is by playing multiplayer games with only one person (you)

involved. This allows you to take all the experience and equipment, and is not very difficult. In order to do this, you don't need to use your ISP or connect to Battle.net. All you have to do is choose "Multiplayer Game" from the menu, create a character, and then start a modem or direct connection game.
