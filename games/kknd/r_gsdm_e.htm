However, KKnD does include a cool new feature that marks an improvement over current real-time strategies. In this game, you have the ability to build multiple units by giving a simple order, so you no longer have to spend time clicking once every 2 seconds trying to build up a small army of minigunners. You can either set the computer to build, say, five ATV's and stop, or actually keep building indefinitely until you order it to halt.

 Single and Multiplayer

The box boasts KKnD to incorporate one of the best AI's in real-time strategies yet. I have my doubts. It's true that the missions are incredibly hard, but this may not be down to a clever AI. After all, the computer often starts the game with a base and a heavily outnumbering amount of troops, so he always gets the advantage - the fact that he will continually send in small attacks, sometimes charge in on different sides (but rarely at the same time) doesn't really demonstrate

anything we haven't seen before. Units do occasionally retreat when reduced from a large group to just one or two, but this is hardly ground-breaking. What makes the missions so annoying is that you can rarely win one until you've played it over 10 times, studied the map completely, and then worked out an unbeatable strategy; and even then, that might not be good enough!
